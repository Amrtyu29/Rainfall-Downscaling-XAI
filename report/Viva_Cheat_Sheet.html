Open with: “I built an ML system that turns coarse climate data into high-resolution rainfall maps for India — and it explains its own predictions with SHAP. I then extended it to two things never done for India: explaining future rainfall change, and explaining what drives flood-level days.”

PART 1 Downscale + Explain

Do: 7 ERA5 predictors (humidity, wind, pressure, temp) → IMD rainfall at 0.25°, 1980–2023. “Point-to-pixel”: each month×cell = 1 row (~9M rows). Compared 4 models.

Result: LightGBM R²=0.73 vs linear 0.21 → ML ~triples the skill.
SHAP: humidity drives rain everywhere; model learned real physics — even cyclones on the Odisha coast, on its own.
“Not just accurate — SHAP proves it learned the monsoon’s physics, so we can trust it.”

PART 2 · NOVEL Future (CMIP6)

Do: feed the trained model bias-corrected future climate (2 GCMs, scenarios SSP2-4.5 & SSP5-8.5).

Proof it’s valid: driven by past climate data it reproduces the real monsoon — r=0.965, bias +3.6%.

Result: monsoon gets wetter, +17% to +49% (2040–70). Both GCMs agree.
Why (SHAP): rising humidity drives it; temp/pressure oppose weakly → “moistening beats weakening.”
“Most studies just report the change — we’re first to explain WHY, region by region.”

PART 3 · NOVEL Flood Days

Do: switch to daily data. Predict flood-level days (≥64.5 mm/day) from large-scale fields with an XGBoost classifier.

Result: ROC-AUC 0.86 on unseen years (heavy days only 1.5% → ~10× over chance).
Mechanism switch: totals = humidity; floods = 500 hPa circulation (monsoon depressions), NOT humidity.
“Thermodynamics sets the stage, but dynamics delivers the flood.”

💬 If sir asks…

Why test on 2016–2023?
Split by time, not randomly — tested on years never seen, so no leakage/cheating.
LightGBM best, but SHAP on XGBoost — why?
Skill is near-identical; XGBoost gives exact, fast TreeSHAP explanations.
Isn’t this the China/Turkey paper?
Same idea, first for India — plus future projections & extremes, which they never did.
How do you trust the future run?
The r=0.965 historical validation licenses it before extrapolating.
Why does the monsoon get wetter?
Warmer air holds more moisture (Clausius–Clapeyron); SHAP confirms humidity is the driver.
